Q. If you were to mentor someone new in web development, what advice would you give them?

ANSWER:-
If I were mentoring someone new in web development, I’d start by advising them to focus on the basics first, like HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Mastering these core skills is important because it makes learning frameworks like React or Next.js much easier. I’d also suggest building small projects from scratch—such as a personal website or a simple to-do list app—because hands-on experience is the best way to learn.

Once they’re comfortable, I’d recommend getting involved in open-source projects. Contributing to open source is a great way to show their skills to others, learn from experienced developers, and build connections in the tech community. They can start small by fixing bugs or adding features, and over time, they’ll gain confidence and valuable experience. 

Finally, I’d remind them to stay curious and open to feedback, as web development is always changing. Being willing to learn and improve will help them grow and succeed in their career.
